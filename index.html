General Information about South Korea

Here you can learn some basic information about South Korea and the purpose of this site!

The Korean Language

The official language of Korea is Korean (Wikipedia contributors, 2020a). Korean just like many other languages, has many dialects unique to a region of South Korea; this might make it hard for visitors who don’t have a strong grasp of the Korean language (Wikipedia contributors, 2020a). Another thing that is unique about Korean is that it has honorifics, when talking to someone with higher status, the speaker with lower status must speak using special nouns or verb endings to highlight the difference in status (Wikipedia contributors, 2020a). Honorifics in the past used to very hierarchical but nowadays is mostly used to show respect typically to individuals older than the speaker, employer, teacher, customers, and other similar individuals (Wikipedia contributors, 2020a). The written system in Korean is called Hangul and was created by King Sejong to help promote literacy among the common people as back then Chinese characters were used (Wikipedia contributors, 2020a). Hangul was actually denounced by the Yangban, Korean high class, due to it being easy to learn but nonetheless became widespread (Wikipedia contributors, 2020a).

South Korean History

The history of Korea is long and would require extensive explanation, hence only the colonial period of South Korean history will be explained here. In 1910, the empire of Japan annexed Korea through the Japan-Korea Annexation Treaty (Wikipedia contributors, 2020b). During this annexation, many traditional Korean customs and the Korean language were suppressed, and economic policies were implemented primarily for Japanese benefits (Wikipedia contributors, 2020b). The annexation ended after World War 2 after Japan surrounded to the Allied Forces; however this led to the split of the Korean peninsula with the Soviet Union taking over the north of the 38th parallel and the United States taking over the south of the 38th parallel (Wikipedia contributors, 2020b). On 1950, the Korean War broke out which devastated both the North and the South; with led to an armistice which still exists to this day (Wikipedia contributors, 2020b). This devastated the South Korean economy but since the 1950s and then on South Korean economy gained incredible growth now known as the Miracle of the Han River (Wikipedia contributors, 2020b). Nowadays, South Korea is well known for its pop culture which is booming abroad, this phenomenon often referred to as the Korean Wave (Wikipedia contributors, 2020b).
